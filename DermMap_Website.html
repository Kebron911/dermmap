Step 1 — Select Patient

Search by name, DOB, or MRN. Or let your EHR schedule pull today's appointments automatically. Recent patients are one tap away. The patient card shows their last visit date and number of tracked lesions.

Step 2 — Tap the Body Map

A full anatomical diagram — anterior, posterior, lateral, head detail. Tap where the lesion is. Pinch to zoom for precision on hands, feet, or face. A marker pulses to confirm placement. Prior visit markers appear in muted colors so MAs can see the full lesion history at a glance.

Step 3 — Snap a Photo

The camera opens instantly with a circular framing guide for consistent shots across visits. Auto-capture fires when the image is stable and in-focus. The photo attaches to the marker automatically. No naming. No filing. No manual linking. Ever.

Step 4 — Document in Seconds

A bottom sheet slides up with structured fields: size (1–6mm+ with custom entry), shape, color, border, symmetry, and action. Quick-tap options, not free text. Sensible defaults so the MA can move on without slowing down. Save and next.

Step 5 — Visit Complete

One tap. The visit summary shows every documented lesion, flags any for biopsy review, and queues the record for the physician to sign off. A PDF clinical document is generated automatically — ready to drop into any EHR as a document attachment.

Features

Everything built for one workflow:
dermatology documentation in the exam room.

Every feature in DermMap exists because of a specific exam-room problem. Nothing is there for a demo. Everything is there because an MA needed it.

🗺️

Anatomical Body Map

A precise, full-body SVG diagram — anterior, posterior, left and right lateral, head/face detail, hands, and feet. Tap to place a marker at the exact lesion location. Pinch to zoom for precision on small areas. Prior visit markers are visible in a distinct color so MAs can see existing documented lesions at a glance. New markers pulse to confirm placement.

📸

Clinical Photo Capture

The camera opens the instant a marker is placed. A circular framing guide ensures consistent framing across visits — critical for meaningful longitudinal comparison. Auto-capture fires when the image is stable and in-focus. Multiple photos per marker for macro and dermoscopic shots. Photos attach automatically — no manual linking step, ever.

🔬

Dermoscopy Lens Support

Compatible with DermLite, 3Gen, and Canfield lens adapters. The app detects the attached lens and adjusts exposure and white balance automatically. Dermoscopic and clinical photos are stored separately and labeled accordingly. No manual tagging required.

⚡

Structured Documentation

A bottom sheet with quick-tap fields: size (1–6mm+ with custom entry), shape, color, border, symmetry, and action (monitor, biopsy scheduled, biopsy performed, excision, referral). Sensible defaults let MAs skip unneeded fields without slowing down. Free-text clinical notes available to providers only.

📡

Offline Mode

Full offline operation with local AES-256 encryption. Mutations are queued and replayed automatically on reconnect with conflict resolution. Designed as a first-class feature, not a fallback. The app works completely normally — body map, photo capture, documentation — with zero connectivity.

📅

Longitudinal Lesion Tracking

Every lesion builds a complete history: photo carousel, side-by-side comparison, structured data changes over time, biopsy results, and pathology notes. Status indicators show at a glance what's being monitored, what's been biopsied, and what's been removed. The feature that catches the changing mole.

📄

PDF Export

One-tap generation of a PDF/A-compliant clinical document: patient info, visit date, body map with numbered markers, per-lesion photo grid, structured data table, and provider signature line. Clinical-quality photos embedded. Target file size under 5MB. Compatible with every EHR as a document attachment — no API required.

👥

Role-Based Access

Three distinct roles — Medical Assistant, Physician, Practice Manager — each with precisely scoped permissions. No user sees data outside their role. No shared logins. Session timeout with biometric/PIN resume. Break-glass emergency access with mandatory justification logging.

📊

Audit Log

Immutable, searchable log of every system action — created, viewed, updated, exported, logged in, logged out. Retained 6 years per HIPAA requirements. Exportable to CSV. Searchable and filterable by user, date, action type, and affected record. Your compliance officer will love it.

📈

Practice Analytics Dashboard

Visits per day/week/month, average documentation time, photos per visit, provider adoption metrics, and an ROI calculator that converts visit volume and MA hourly cost into projected time savings. Understand your practice's documentation health at a glance.

Common questions

Does DermMap replace our EHR?

No. DermMap works alongside your existing EHR. The PDF export drops directly into any EHR as a document attachment — the same way you'd attach a lab result. No IT project required on day one.

How long does setup take?

Most practices are fully configured and live within 48 hours. There is no IT involvement required. An MA can be documenting in the exam room the same week you sign up.

Are there any long-term contracts?

Monthly plans have no commitment — cancel anytime. Annual plans receive a 10% discount and are invoiced upfront with a 60-day renewal notice window. We do not use evergreen contracts.

Is there a HIPAA Business Associate Agreement (BAA) available?

Yes. A signed BAA is available on all plans. For Enterprise customers it is included automatically. For Solo, Practice, and Group customers, contact us to request one.
