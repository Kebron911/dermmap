## 1. Brand Foundation

### Mission
Eliminate documentation burden from the dermatology exam room.

### Brand Promise
Lesion documented in 8 seconds. On any phone. In any exam room.

### Positioning Statement
DermMap is the only lesion documentation platform built from the ground up for the Medical Assistant's hand during the exam — not the physician's desk after it.

### Brand Personality
**Clinically precise. Refreshingly direct. Purpose-built.**

DermMap earns trust by being *specific*, not aspirational. Every claim carries a number. The brand does not use marketing language — it uses clinical language applied to a business problem.

- **Not:** "A powerful, intuitive platform that seamlessly integrates with your workflow."
- **Yes:** "Document a skin lesion in 8 seconds. On any phone. In any exam room."

### What DermMap Is Not
- A generic EHR — it complements existing EHRs, never competes with them
- A consumer health app — it is a clinical tool used by licensed professionals
- An enterprise software product — it is purpose-built for a single specialty workflow
- A platform for everything — it solves one problem with precision

## 2. Target Audience

DermMap has three distinct audiences with different purchase motivations. Marketing and product language must address all three without flattening them.

### Medical Assistant (MA) — Primary User
- **Who:** The person doing documentation in the exam room
- **Pain:** Being the bottleneck — hand-writing notes, filing photos manually, staying late to enter data
- **Win:** Finishing charts before the physician walks in
- **Language register:** Practical, fast, respectful of their time. Never clinical-academic.
- **Key messages:** Speed, ease of use, one-handed operation, offline mode

### Dermatologist / Provider — Clinical Champion
- **Who:** The physician who evaluates and signs off on documentation
- **Pain:** No visual record of lesion history; hunting through photos across visits; missing the mole that changed
- **Win:** Complete longitudinal view in one tap; the catch that prevents a missed diagnosis
- **Language register:** Clinical precision, professional, data-forward
- **Key messages:** Longitudinal tracking, comparison tools, change detection, sign-off workflow

### Practice Manager / Owner — Economic Buyer
- **Who:** The person who signs the contract
- **Pain:** $7,000+/provider/year in unbillable MA time; no visibility into documentation quality; compliance exposure
- **Win:** Measurable ROI, defensible compliance posture, fast onboarding
- **Language register:** Business metrics, compliance, operational efficiency
- **Key messages:** ROI calculator, 96% adoption rate, 48-hour onboarding, audit log, pricing transparency

## 3. Messaging Architecture

### The Core Four Pillars

Every piece of DermMap communication leads with one or more of these pillars. They are listed in priority order for cold outreach; adjust order based on audience (see Section 2).

| # | Pillar | Core Claim | Proof Point |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Speed** | Document a lesion in 8 seconds | vs. 4–6 min industry average; timed in demos |
| 2 | **Purpose-built** | Designed for the exam room, not forced into it | Phone-first architecture; one-handed; offline-first |
| 3 | **Compliance** | HIPAA built in, not bolted on | AES-256, TLS 1.3, immutable 6-yr audit log, no camera roll |
| 4 | **ROI** | Measurable, immediate, calculable | $7,000+/provider/yr recovered; 96% MA adoption |

### Hero Statistic
**8 seconds.** This is the brand's single most important number. It appears in the hero headline, on-screen during demos, in the navigation CTA area, and in the stat bar. Never round it to "under 10 seconds" in hero placements — "8 seconds" is more credible because it is specific.

### Supporting Statistics (always cite these with context)
- 96% MA adoption rate after training
- 6+ minutes saved per patient
- $7,000+ per provider per year in recovered productivity
- 48-hour typical onboarding
- 100% HIPAA compliant by design
- AES-256 encryption / TLS 1.3 / 6-year audit retention

### The "Against" Frame
DermMap's problem framing is its strongest sales tool. Always name the alternative:

> *"Dermatology clinics average 4–6 minutes of unbillable time per patient on skin documentation — hunting for old photos, typing descriptors into an EHR not designed for derm... Multiply that by your daily visit volume. The number is uncomfortable."*

This pattern — name the pain, quantify it, make the math uncomfortable, then offer the solution — is the brand's core narrative structure.

## 4. Voice & Tone

### Voice Characteristics

**Direct.** Say the thing. No throat-clearing, no preamble. "Document a skin lesion in 8 seconds." Not "We've built a platform that enables faster dermatology documentation workflows."

**Specific.** Numbers over adjectives. Concrete over abstract. "8 seconds" not "fast." "$7,000+" not "significant savings." "96%" not "most MAs."

**Confident without arrogance.** DermMap makes strong claims because it can back them up. It does not hedge. But it also tells prospects when it's not the right fit ("if DermMap isn't the right fit, we'll tell you").

**Clinical, not corporate.** Language should feel like it belongs in a clinic, not a boardroom. "Exam room," not "point-of-care environment." "Physician," not "provider stakeholder." "MA," not "clinical documentation staff."

**Anti-marketing.** The brand earns trust by sounding like the opposite of a sales pitch.

### Banned Words & Phrases

Never use these — they trigger the "sounds like every other software company" response:

| Banned | Replace with |
|---|---|
| Seamless | Specific workflow description |
| Intuitive | Specific speed/ease claim |
| Powerful | Specific capability |
| Robust | Specific technical spec |
| Leverages | Uses |
| Revolutionizes | Specific before/after comparison |
| Best-in-class | Specific differentiator |
| Cutting-edge | Specific technical detail |
| Streamline | Specific time/step reduction |
| Solution | The actual product name |

### Tone by Context

| Context | Tone | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Hero headline | Bold, confrontational, specific | *"Document a skin lesion in 8 seconds."* |
| Problem framing | Empathetic but frank | *"Your current workflow costs you more than you think."* |
| Feature description | Precise, clinical, brief | *"A full anatomical SVG — anterior, posterior, lateral. Tap to place. Pinch to zoom."* |
| Security/compliance | Authoritative, technically specific | *"AES-256 at rest, TLS 1.3 in transit, immutable 6-year audit trail."* |
| Demo CTA | Direct, no pressure | *"See it in 20 minutes. No slides. The actual app, timed."* |
| App UI (toasts, confirms) | Helpful, brief, clinical | *"Visit locked. PDF queued."* |
| Pricing | Transparent, no-surprise | *"No hidden fees. No per-patient charges. No surprise overages."* |
| Error states | Clear, actionable | Specific problem + specific next step |

### Exemplary Copy (from existing assets — use as reference)

These lines are the brand voice at its best. Use them as the quality bar:

- *"Built for the exam room. Not forced into it."*
- *"HIPAA compliance you can actually explain to your compliance officer."*
- *"Ten purpose-built features. Zero unnecessary ones."*
- *"No hidden fees. No per-patient charges. No surprise overages."*
- *"Exam rooms have bad WiFi. We planned for that."*
- *"HIPAA isn't a feature. It's the foundation."*
- *"A DermMap demo is a live walkthrough — no slides, no decks."*
- *"Zero sales pressure — if it's not a fit, we'll tell you."*
- *"The clock doesn't lie."*

## 5. Visual Identity

### Design Philosophy

The visual system follows a three-zone rhythm:

1. **Dark hero zone** — Navy (`#0B2535`) with teal radial glow. Conveys authority, trust, precision. Used for hero sections and dark-mode components.
2. **Clean white zone** — Near-white (`#F8FAF9`) backgrounds. Conveys clarity, clinical cleanliness. Used for feature and content sections.
3. **Editorial warmth zone** — Instrument Serif for display text, warm photography/illustration. Prevents the product from feeling cold or purely utilitarian.

**Rule:** The sequence is always Dark → White. Never reverse it within a page.

### Layout Principles

- Max content width: `1200px`
- Section vertical padding: `88px` top and bottom
- Container horizontal padding: `28px`
- Card border radius: `8px` (standard), `12px` (featured/elevated)
- Navigation height: `68px` fixed

## 6. Typography System

### Font Stack

| Role | Font Family | Fallback | Weight Range |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Display** | Instrument Serif | Georgia, Times New Roman, serif | 400 (normal), 400 italic |
| **Body / UI** | Outfit | system-ui, -apple-system, sans-serif | 300, 400, 500, 600, 700 |
| **Data / Mono** | Space Mono | Courier New, monospace | 400, 700 |

**Source:** Google Fonts — all three are loaded via `fonts.googleapis.com`.

### When to Use Each

**Instrument Serif** — High-stakes, emotional, authoritative claims. Used for:
- Primary `h2` section headlines
- Logo wordmark (italic)
- Editorial pull-quotes and testimonials
- "Why DermMap" type narrative headers

**Outfit** — All functional text. Used for:
- Body copy and section introductions
- Navigation links
- Button labels
- Form labels and inputs
- Feature descriptions
- All UI text inside the app

**Space Mono** — Numbers and technical labels. Used for:
- Section eyebrow labels (`PURPOSE-BUILT DERMATOLOGY DOCUMENTATION`)
- Key statistics in stat bars (`8s`, `96%`, `$7k+`)
- Technical specs (encryption standards, version numbers)
- Code and API documentation

### Type Scale

| Element | Font | Size | Weight | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| `h1` (hero) | Outfit | `clamp(2.8rem, 6vw, 4.5rem)` | 700 | Letter-spacing: `-0.02em` |
| `h2` (section) | Instrument Serif | `clamp(1.9rem, 4vw, 3rem)` | 400 | Line-height: `1.15`, letter-spacing: `-0.01em` |
| `h3` (card/sub) | Outfit | `1.15rem` | 600 | Line-height: `1.35` |
| Body / `p` | Outfit | `1rem` | 400 | Line-height: `1.65` |
| Section intro | Outfit | `1.05rem` | 400 | Color: `--text-muted`, max-width: `680px`, line-height: `1.75` |
| Section label | Space Mono | `10.5px` | 700 | Uppercase, letter-spacing: `0.14em`, color: `--teal` |

### Status / Clinical Colors

These colors have direct clinical meaning in the product. Do not repurpose them for decorative use.

| Token | Hex | Clinical Meaning | UI Meaning |
|---|---|---|---|
| `--amber` | `#F59E0B` | — | Warning, demo banner, atypical flag |
| `--amber-light` | `#FEF3C7` | — | Amber wash backgrounds |
| `--red` | `#EF4444` | Malignant / urgent | Error states, destructive actions |
| `--red-light` | `#FEE2E2` | — | Error wash backgrounds |
| `--green` | `#10B981` | Excised / resolved | Success states, locked visits |
| `--orange` | `#F97316` | Biopsied / pending | Pending biopsy status indicators |

### Color Usage Rules

1. **Teal is the only brand color.** All CTAs, links, active states, and brand moments use teal. It should not compete with clinical status colors.
2. **Amber = alert only.** Never use amber for decorative purposes. It signals "something needs attention." In the app, it is reserved for the demo banner and atypical lesion flags.
3. **Red = clinical urgency or errors.** Never use red decoratively.
4. **Dark navy hero is non-negotiable.** The product's authority comes partly from the dark-to-light visual contrast. Lightening the hero background would erode brand gravity.
5. **Background is warm white, not pure white.** `#F8FAF9` has a slight warm/cool tint. Pure `#FFFFFF` is for card surfaces only.

## 8. Logo & Wordmark

### Logotype
The DermMap logo is a **typographic wordmark** in Instrument Serif italic, followed by an animated pulse dot.

```
DermMap •
         ↑ teal pulse animation
```

### Wordmark Specifications

| Property | Value |
|---|---|
| Typeface | Instrument Serif, italic |
| Font size (nav) | `1.5rem` |
| Color (on dark) | `#FFFFFF` |
| Color (on light) | `#0B2535` (`--dark`) |
| Letter spacing | `-0.02em` |

### Pulse Dot Specifications

| Property | Value |
|---|---|
| Size | `8px × 8px` |
| Shape | Circle (`border-radius: 50%`) |
| Color | `#14B8A6` (`--teal-bright`) |
| Gap from wordmark | `10px` |
| Animation | `pulse-ring` keyframe, `2.2s ease-out infinite` |
| Animation detail | Box shadow expands from `0` to `8px radius` at `rgba(20,184,166,0.5)` → fades to transparent |

### Pulse Animation Keyframes
```css
@keyframes pulse-ring {
  0%   { box-shadow: 0 0 0 0   rgba(20,184,166,0.5); }
  70%  { box-shadow: 0 0 0 8px rgba(20,184,166,0);   }
  100% { box-shadow: 0 0 0 0   rgba(20,184,166,0);   }
}
```

### Logo Meaning
The pulse dot is a deliberate clinical signal — it evokes a heartbeat monitor or a live status indicator, reinforcing that DermMap is a real-time clinical tool, not a static records system.

### Logo Don'ts
- Do not use the wordmark without the pulse dot in digital contexts
- Do not use the logo in teal on a teal background
- Do not use Outfit (body font) for the wordmark — it must be Instrument Serif italic
- Do not remove the italic — the slant is part of the mark's personality

### Logo Asset Gap ⚠️
As of v1.0, the logo exists only as inline HTML/CSS. **There is no standalone SVG logo file.** This must be created before any sales decks, email signatures, print materials, or third-party placements. Priority: high.

## 9. Motion & Animation

### Easing Functions

| Token | Value | Use |
|---|---|---|
| `--ease` | `cubic-bezier(0.16, 1, 0.3, 1)` | Default — smooth, slightly springy exits |
| `--ease-in` | `cubic-bezier(0.4, 0, 1, 1)` | Elements leaving the screen |

### Timing Scale

| Token | Duration | Use |
|---|---|---|
| `--fast` | `150ms` | Hover states, focus rings, small color transitions |
| `--base` | `280ms` | Standard transitions (nav scroll, card hovers, reveals) |
| `--slow` | `480ms` | Section reveals, large entrance animations |

### Animation Principles

1. **Purposeful only.** Animation communicates state change or draws attention to something important. Never decorative.
2. **Exits are fast; entrances can be deliberate.** A 150ms hover response feels responsive. A 480ms page section reveal feels premium.
3. **Counter-based number animation** — stat bar numbers count up from 0 to their target value on scroll. This is the only "celebration" animation on the marketing site and it reinforces the brand's data credibility.
4. **Scroll-reveal pattern** — `.reveal` elements animate in from slight y-offset with opacity 0→1. Staggered via `data-delay` attributes (0ms, 100ms, 200ms increments).
5. **Button micro-interactions** — `transform: translateY(-1px)` on hover, `translateY(0)` on active. Subtle lift conveys tactility.

## 10. UI Component Standards

### Navigation

- Fixed position, `68px` height
- Background: `rgba(11, 37, 53, 0.92)` + `backdrop-filter: blur(12px)`
- On scroll: darkens to `rgba(11, 37, 53, 0.98)` + adds bottom shadow
- Links: `0.875rem` Outfit 500, `rgba(255,255,255,0.72)`, hover → white
- CTA button ("Demo"): filled teal, `#0D9488`, `font-weight: 600`, `border-radius: 7px`

### Buttons

Three button variants — use the correct one by context:

| Variant | Class | Background | Text | Use |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Primary | `.btn-primary` | `#0D9488` teal | White | Primary action on any background |
| Secondary | `.btn-secondary` | `rgba(255,255,255,0.1)` glass | White | Secondary action on dark backgrounds only |
| Outline | `.btn-outline` | Transparent | Teal | Secondary action on light backgrounds |

All buttons:
- `padding: 13px 26px`
- `border-radius: 8px`
- `font-size: 0.9375rem`, `font-weight: 600`
- Hover: `translateY(-1px)` lift
- Primary hover: deeper teal `#0F766E` + stronger shadow

### Cards

- Background: `#FFFFFF`
- Border: `1px solid #E2E8F0` (`--border`)
- Border radius: `8px`
- Hover: slight lift (`translateY(-2px)`) + shadow increase
- Featured/highlighted variant: teal left border accent or teal header background

### Section Labels (eyebrows)

The small uppercase Space Mono label above section headlines. This is a critical brand pattern — every major section should have one.

- Font: Space Mono, `10.5px`, `font-weight: 700`
- Transform: `uppercase`
- Letter spacing: `0.14em`
- Color: `--teal` (`#0D9488`)
- Margin-bottom: `14px`

Example values seen across assets:
- "Purpose-built dermatology documentation"
- "Why DermMap"
- "How it works"
- "For physicians"
- "Security & compliance"
- "Works alongside your EHR"
- "For practice managers"
- "Pricing"
- "Request a demo"
- "All features"

### Hero Section Layout

- Min-height: `100vh`
- Background: `--dark` (`#0B2535`) + radial teal glow (top-right) + subtle dot texture
- Two-column grid at desktop: content left, visual/mockup right
- On mobile: stacked, content first
- Eyebrow tag → H1 → subheadline → CTA group → trust bar

### Trust Bar / Social Proof Bar

Below the hero CTA. Three-item horizontal bar:
- "Trusted by dermatology practices"
- "HIPAA Compliant"
- "Works alongside your EHR"

Separated by `·` dividers. Muted text color. Signals credibility without overselling.

### Stat Bar

Four stat cards with animated counters:
- `8s` — Avg. documentation time
- `96%` — MA adoption rate after training
- `6+` — Minutes saved per patient
- `100%` — HIPAA compliant by design

Numbers rendered in Space Mono, large display size. Labels in Outfit muted.

### Comparison Layout

Side-by-side two-column layout contrasting "Traditional EHR Workflow" (❌) vs. "DermMap Workflow" (✅). This is the highest-converting section pattern and should be preserved in any redesign.

## 12. Known Gaps & Future Work

These are brand deficiencies as of v1.0. Prioritized by impact.

### Priority 1 — Must Resolve

**1. No standalone SVG logo asset**
The logo exists only as inline HTML/CSS. Required for: sales decks, email signatures, print materials, social media profiles, partner co-branding, any context outside the website. Deliverable: SVG file in at least two variants (white-on-dark, dark-on-light), with and without pulse dot for static contexts.

**2. Logo markup inconsistency across website pages**
`index.html` uses `.nav-logo` with `.logo-text` span + `.logo-pulse` span — correct.
`pricing.html` and `security.html` use a different `.logo` div with `.logo-text` + `.pulse-dot` — slightly different class naming.
`features.html` and `demo.html` render the wordmark as plain text inside `.nav-logo` or `.nav-logo` div without a pulse dot span at all.
All five pages must use identical logo markup.

**3. No micro-copy brand guide for the app UI**
The app's toast messages, empty states, confirmation dialogs, and error messages have no defined voice standard. They should match the directness of the marketing site. Examples needed for:
- Visit locked confirmation toast
- Offline mode activation
- Photo capture success
- Session timeout warning
- Error states (network, validation, permission)

### Priority 2 — Should Resolve

**4. App typography divergence**
The app uses Tailwind's default Tailwind CSS font stack (system fonts). The marketing site uses Instrument Serif + Outfit + Space Mono. The app should adopt at minimum Outfit for body text and Space Mono for data/stats to maintain typographic continuity between marketing and product.

**5. No brand color for the app's "demo mode" state**
The demo banner uses amber correctly. But the demo data, demo user roles, and demo environment have no consistent visual language beyond the banner itself. Define a clear "you are in demo mode" system.

**6. No defined illustration or photography style**
The website uses CSS/SVG-generated visuals (phone mockup, body outline, workflow panels). This works for the prototype but will need a real illustration or photography direction as the brand matures. Suggestion: line-art clinical illustration style with teal accent color, warm skin tone palette — matching the body map's existing aesthetic.

### Priority 3 — Nice to Have

**7. Dark mode specification**
The app is light-mode only. The marketing site is dark-hero + light-body. No dark mode spec exists for the product UI. Define before it becomes a support request.

**8. Accessibility brand statement**
ACCESSIBILITY.md exists but accessibility is not stated as a brand value anywhere in the marketing copy. "Built for the exam room" should include "accessible to every clinician in it." Add to brand pillars when copy is next refreshed.

**9. Favicon and app icon specification**
No favicon or PWA icon spec documented. Should be: teal circle with white "D" or stylized pulse mark. Define dimensions and variants (16px, 32px, 180px, 512px).
